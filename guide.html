Diversity and Design
Cultural Diversity = Creative Diversity

"Diversity and inclusion within the field of design lead to more innovation through problem-solving, whether in service to business or society. And that’s what design is all about. In order to continue successfully and authentically leading improvement and change, design must openly embrace more diverse practitioners and retain them through inclusion." [8]

Approach to multicultural design —Cultural Sensitivity

Cultural sensitivity is being aware that cultural differences and similarities between people exist without assigning them a value – positive or negative, better or worse, right or wrong. [9]

Appreciation or Appropriation?

French Luxury Fashion brand Dior launched its 2020 Resort Collection, which showcases Western reintrepretation of African wax fabric.

Keys to success

Collaboration & Extensive Research:
Dior published behind-the-scenes photos of designer Maria Grazia Chiuri working in collaboration with local artisans and designers. Their active collaboration and thorough research demonstrate Dior's respect and honor to the African culture and tradition.

Re-defining the "Couture":
The collection extends the Western conception of "couture" to the elegance of human labor and the touch of the human hand, all of which are evident in the African artisan culture and traditions. [10]

The Guide
The guide is to help you prepare and avoid unintentional cultural appropriation in your future design projects.

With this guide, you will:

  • Consolidate your motivation and goal for the project.
  • Assess your understanding of the context and the controversy around the culture.
  • Evaluate the validity of your research and resources.
Q1
Where did I first encounter this particular cultural material?
Q2
Why did I chose to borrow from this particular culture?
Q3
What am I trying to achieve through the project?
Q4
Why do I need this particular cultural material in my project?
Q5
What are the controversies surrounding this cultural material?
Q6
Have I found accurate or credible sources?
what's next?
Research more

It is extremely important that you have a thorough understanding of the cultural and historical contexts. If you were unsure how to answer any of the questions above, you will need to find more sources.

Reach out to people

Don't just rely on second-hand sources. If you happen to know someone who's part of the culture you're interested in, ask them for opinions and feedback.

Come back to the guide anytime

While working on the project, keep asking yourself about your motivation and goal for the project.
